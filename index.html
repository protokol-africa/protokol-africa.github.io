The netting thesis
On any bilateral corridor with two-way traffic, 80-90% of gross volume nets out. You only need to convert the residual. Applied across all African corridors simultaneously, multilateral netting could reduce actual settlement volume — and costs — by an order of magnitude.

FAQ

Common questions.

Is this a cryptocurrency?
No. Protokol uses blockchain (Stellar) as a neutral settlement ledger between operators — not as a currency. End users never see or touch crypto. Every digital unit in the protocol is backed 1:1 by real fiat currency held in regulated bank accounts.
Do operators need to change their systems?
No. Operators connect via a single API. They keep their apps, their agents, their customers, their brand. Protokol handles only the invisible settlement layer between them. An agent in Dakar doesn't even know the protocol exists.
Who pays and how much?
Operators pay 0.05-0.10% per transaction — 5 to 20 times less than current settlement solutions. Pay-per-use only, no minimum, no setup fee. Zero transactions means zero cost.
Is this competing with PAPSS?
No. PAPSS connects banks through central bank infrastructure. Protokol connects fintechs and Mobile Money operators through an open protocol. The two are complementary — different layers serving different participants in the same ecosystem.
